Organizational Profile

Who We Are

NGROPPMAN serves as the umbrella organization representing stakeholders along the entire groundnut value chain, including smallholder farmers, commercial producers, aggregators, processors, input suppliers, exporters, traders, researchers, financial institutions, and development partners.

From a strategic perspective, NGROPPMAN has the potential to evolve beyond a commodity association into a nationally recognized commodity-development institution. The rebranding pathway is: Build the Association → Activate the Programmes → Attract Investments.

Federal Government Proposal

National Aflatoxin Contamination Control Program

NGROPPMAN proposes a 3-year, nationwide control program targeting 100,000 farmers to eliminate this critical threat to Nigeria's groundnut industry.

Project Activities

📚
Farmer Capacity Building
Nationwide training workshops, extension materials in local languages, and demonstration farms.
🛠️
Aflatoxin Control Technologies
Solar drying tarpaulins, moisture meters, hermetic storage bags distributed to beneficiaries.
🔬
Testing & Certification Centers
Regional aflatoxin testing laboratories, mobile rapid testing units, and certification systems.
📢
Awareness Campaigns
Radio and media outreach, stakeholder sensitization, consumer education on food safety risks.

Cluster Management System

One Cluster — One Business Plan — One Prosperous Community

The NGROPPMAN Cluster Management System organizes members into geographically defined, commercially viable groups across all 36 states and FCT.

🏛️
National Level
National Cluster Coordination Committee (NCCC)
Policy direction · National planning
🏢
State Level
State Cluster Management Committee (SCMC)
Coordinate clusters · Capacity building
🏘️
LGA Level
LGA Cluster Committee
Member mobilization · Input distribution
🌾
Community Level
Groundnut Enterprise Cluster (GEC)
100–500 Farmers · 20–50 Processors

📦 Commodity Aggregation

Each cluster establishes collection centres, warehouses, quality inspection units, and digital weighing systems. Minimum target: 5,000 MT annually per mature cluster.

🏭 Processing Functions

Cleaning, sorting, shelling, oil extraction, cake production, packaging, branding, and quality certification. At least one processing hub per Senatorial District.

💻 Digital Management

Every member receives a unique membership number, digital ID card, and access to market prices, weather alerts, input requests, loan applications, and extension services.

Phase I (2026)
Pilot clusters in 12 priority states
Phase II (2027–2028)
Expansion to all 36 states & FCT
Phase III (2029–2031)
Commodity exchanges & digital trading
